Problem

Phoenix area utility workers have used a virtual content and document repository for years to access important files and informaiton about power and water plants. The site requires a massive learning curves and contains many dark UI patterns and hidden information. Information architecture is a mess and newer recruits are getting lost in the process of trying to identify a document or hunt down specs.

How do we redesign this repository so information is easier to locate?

Navigating the current product - blocks in experience.

  • Link Repository
    Stakeholders and users alike pointed towards the LMS functioning as nothing but a link repository, which relied on blocks of content to direct and teach students.
  • Duplicated Information
    Getting around the LMS was not cohesive. Navigating courses and programs was confusing and often mislead students.
  • No Deliniations
    Many of the elements within the LMS are static. Students are expecting an interactive experience.
  • Broken Processes
    LMS has major accessibility issues. It is also lacking proper CC and transcripts.
  • Accessibility
    Audit of information architecture is needed to reinstate hierarchies and normalize flows across the student experience.
